Perception - Openness/Intellect and Conscientiousness

Fanciful: They tend to be imaginative, creative, innovative, and somewhat impractical.

Visionary: Future-focused. They have big ideas and will drive towards achieving them. May struggle living in the moment.

Routinized: They tend to be methodical, consistent, and organized. May struggle with change.

Immediate: They are considered as the ultimate realists. Living in the moment but having a hard time with future plans.

Lifestyle - Openness/Intellect and Extraversion

Introspective: Reflective and enjoying alone time. They deeply gather knowledge about the world.

Adventurous: They enjoy trying out new things and experiences.

Mainstream: They easily adapt to groups and mainstream stuff. May disregard individuality.

Predictable: They tend to live a predictable and stable lifestyle. Often staying with the familiar.

Worldview - Openness/Intellect and Agreeableness

Rebellious: Independent and unique, they are considered as the rebels.

Tolerant: Open-minded and accepting of other people's differences.

Traditional: They tend to have an easy time following social norms.

Resolute: They have strong, unchanging beliefs about the world. Decisive yet narrow-minded.

Coping - Openness/Intellect and Neuroticism

Resolving: They find rational solutions to problems. Having a positive attitude towards new things.

Eccentric: Perceptive and sensitive to the world around them. May vividly imagine good or bad things happening to them.

Avoidant: They avoid risks and new things in order to stay safe.

Grounded: They tend to have an easy time controlling emotions and letting their mind shut.

Work Style - Conscientiousness and Extraversion

Impulsive: Taking advantage of present opportunities. Considered as spontaneous.

Ambitious: They work hard with vigor to get things done as quickly as possible.

Steady: Can withstand boring tasks with ease. Preference for planning in advance.

Laid-back: They tend to live life at their own pace. Can be somewhat sluggish.

Interpersonal Style - Conscientiousness and Agreeableness

Drifting: They lack strong opinions. Willing to adapt to others.

Altruistic: Actively helping others in order to preserve harmony.

Systematic: Focused on achievement and can be somewhat competitive.

Egocentric: Willing to do their own thing without worrying about others.

Challenge Approach - Conscientiousness and Neuroticism

Scattered: They tend to have trouble focusing due to unstable moods.

Perfectionistic: Needs to have everything just right to be content.

Confident: They are hard-working and confident about their own abilities.

Relaxed: They take things as they come, and go with the flow without much concern.

Social Style - Extraversion and Agreeableness

Dominant: They prefer taking charge of situations.

Friendly: Often considered as social butterflies who enjoy interacting with people and helping them.

Humble: They tend to be modest and polite. Can put other people above themselves.

Aloof: They tend to be distant loners who have little interest in people.

Temperament - Extraversion and Neuroticism

Upbeat: Optimistic and they generally enjoy life. Can be too content with things. Corresponds to Sanguine temperament.

Excitable: They have excitable and changeable moods. Corresponds to Choleric temeprament.

Somber: They tend to be somewhat gloomy and sees tragedies as they are. Corresponds to Melancholic temperament.

Tranquil: Stable emotions with an even-keeled attitude. Corresponds to Phlegmatic temeprament.

Emotional Management - Agreeableness and Neuroticism

Stable: They trust people easily and tend to be emotionally stable.

Sensitive: Empathetic and sensitive to others' suffering.

Moody: They tend to be skeptical of others. Can be somewhat negative.

Unemotional: They see emotions as a sign of weakness. They take pride in being strong.

Aspect Descriptions

Intellectual Curiosity

High scorers have an hunger for knowledge, enjoy mentally stimulating activities, and feel at home with abstract ideas. Low scorers are typically more physical than intellectual and prefer simple interests or tasks over complex ones.

Unconventionality

High unconventionality means you prioritize imagination, introspection, new experiences, and you are open-minded to alternative and unconventional approaches. A low score means you are more conventional, conservative, and down-to-earth.

Industriousness

People high in this trait are ambitious, goal-oriented, and they will work hard and smart to get things done as efficiently as possible. Low scorers tend to procrastinate and waste time more, and they may prioritize play over work.

Orderliness

Highly orderly people make plans and organize their environment. They are careful about making decisions, and they may be perfectionistic. Low scorers tend to be more messy and spontaneous. They may have a harder time planning but they can be more adaptable.

Enthusiasm

High scorers are enthusiastic, and can be considered the life of the party. They enjoy socializing and have a more cheerful disposition. Low enthusiasm means you are more serious and reserved, and maybe not as interested in socializing.

Assertiveness

People who are highly assertive take charge and lead other people. They can be considered as dominant and persuasive. Low scorers are more likely to take passive roles or work behind-the-scenes. They prefer responding rather than initiating.

Compassion

High compassion means you favor mercy and have a soft heart in general. They are easily affected by others' feelings, and want to help others in many ways. Low score means you are not as sensitive and you tend to prefer justice and rational approaches over mercy.

Politeness

High scorers dislike conflict and they prefer cooperation over competition. They are humble and dislike bragging too much. Low scorers are not as polite and they may be more competitive, manipulative, arrogant, or willing to fight for what they want.

Negative Affect

High scorers are familiar with feelings of doubt, worry, and sadness. They can get caught up in problems or feel embarrased or discouraged easily. Low scorers are not as prone to feeling anxious, depressed, or insecure. They tend to feel more confident.

Emotional Instability

Being high in instability means your emotions are easily affected and volatile. You may feel angry when things are going wrong, have explosive moods, or overindulge in things. Low instability means emotionally stable, calm, and controlled in reaction.
